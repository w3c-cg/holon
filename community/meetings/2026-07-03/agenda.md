# W3C Holon Community Group — Meeting Agenda

**Chair:** Kurt Cagle
**Note:** Candidate minutes from the 19 June inaugural meeting are posted alongside this agenda at `community/meetings/2026-06-19/minutes-candidate.md`. They are a draft pending review — please read before the meeting so item 1 below is a substantive approval rather than a first read.

## Agenda

1. **Approval of 19 June Minutes**
   Review and formal approval of the inaugural meeting minutes. *(Contingent on minutes being drafted and circulated in advance — see note above.)*
   Operational note: with transcription already in place for meetings, a formal minute-taker role isn't required — but the group should name someone (an admin/officer role) responsible for confirming each meeting is actually being recorded and that the transcript is captured and filed. A responsibility, not a task.

2. **Quorum and Voting Rules**
   First substantive decision of the meeting: what constitutes quorum, how votes are called and resolved, and how ties or near-ties are handled. Stated once, in writing, before it's needed rather than improvised live under a contested vote.

3. **Charter: Review and Vote**
   Formal adoption of the charter, deferred from 19 June. As with the IP policy item below, the current charter text should be circulated to the list in advance rather than reviewed live for the first time on the call — same reasoning: anyone voting on an organization's behalf likely needs to clear it internally first.

4. **Acceptance of Proposed Working Groups**
   Brief discussion, then a formal yea/nay vote on the seven proposed working groups: Core Architecture, DataBooks, Network Architecture, Validation/Verification/Security, Industry Utilisation, Hierarchical/Meta-Ontology Alignment, Formal Foundations, and Personalisation/Internationalisation — as posted.

5. **Nominations and Voting for Open Positions**
   - Chair/Co-Chair and working-group-lead nominations, per the process opened on the 19th, including the nomination of Heather Ray for the Admin role.
   - **Dormancy rule:** any working group without a nominated lead goes dormant until a lead comes forward — no groups run leaderless.
   - Informal call for interest: any member willing to act as legal counsel for IP policy review, going forward. *(Formal action item — see #9 below.)*

6. **IP Policy: Ratification of Repository Defaults**
   The `w3c-cg/holon` repository already declares the standard W3C Community Group defaults in [`LICENSE.md`](https://github.com/w3c-cg/holon/blob/main/LICENSE.md): Specification contributions under the [W3C CLA](https://www.w3.org/community/about/process/cla/), other Reports under the W3C Software and Document License, Test Suites under the W3C 3-Clause BSD License. Every current participant has technically already agreed to these terms by joining the group — the vote on the 3rd is to formally **ratify** this as the group's settled IP policy going forward, not to choose between alternatives. Link circulated to the list in advance of the meeting. Brief live discussion to surface any objections (in particular, the CLA's exact patent-scope language is worth a quick confirmation, not just the summary), then a vote — expected to be straightforward given the terms are already operative, but falling back to a short async window (~1 week) if any objection is raised.

7. **Breakout Sessions: Structure and Schedule**
   Proposal to move active discussion into working-group breakout sessions starting **17 July**. The Agent/Subject/Character terminology question is on the preliminary agenda as the first breakout topic — not expected to reach consensus on the 3rd, but intended as a litmus test for how the breakout process itself works.
   Ask of the group: anyone planning to engage with the terminology question should have a written position (even informal — a paragraph to the list) ahead of the 17th, so the breakout starts from committed positions rather than live improvisation.

8. **New Business: Demo Slot Proposal for 17 July**
   Proposing a 15-minute demo slot for the Holon Graph Architecture, framed explicitly as **one candidate implementation, not the reference implementation** — many of the working groups' core issues originate from HGA as a foundational architecture, which is the reason for an early demo, not an endorsement of it as required infrastructure. One or two additional implementation demos to be scheduled within the same time-boxed slot, so this doesn't read as a single-architecture showcase.

9. **AOB (Any Other Business)**
   Open floor for items that don't fit the formal agenda.

10. **Action Items for 17 July**
   - Resolve the legal counsel question (from #5) — who, and confirm the scope of the role (reviewing IP policy language and related materials before they go to vote).
   - Terminology breakout begins, contingent on written positions submitted per #7.
   - HGA + additional implementation demos (15 min each, per #8).
